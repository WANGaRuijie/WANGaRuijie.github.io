We study the fair allocation of indivisible items, where each agent
has a set of relevant items and can only receive items that are
relevant to them. While the relevance constraint has been studied
in recent years, existing work has largely focused on envy-freeness.
In this paper, we complement the literature by providing a relatively
complete understanding of other key fairness criteria — such as
proportionality, equitability, and their relaxations — in settings
where the items may be goods, chores, or a mixture of both.
We argue that for relevance models, proportionality and equi-
tability are often more appropriate fairness criteria, since agents
may find it difficult or impossible to evaluate the items that are not
relevant to them.
